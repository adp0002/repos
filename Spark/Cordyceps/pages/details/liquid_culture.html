üî¨ What is Liquid Culture?

Liquid culture grows fungal mycelium in a nutrient broth. For C. militaris, LC is used to generate vigorous inoculum, produce extracellular metabolites, or stage a two‚Äëphase process (liquid ‚Üí solid fruiting). Unlike solid media, LC emphasizes mass transfer (oxygen, nutrients) and hydrodynamics (shear, mixing).

‚öñÔ∏è Pros & Cons of Liquid Culture

Pros

  • Rapid expansion of inoculum for downstream solid culture
  • Uniform exposure to nutrients; clean sampling
  • Potential for higher extracellular metabolite recovery
  • Scalable from bench flasks to bioreactors

Cons

  • Sensitive to contamination; hard to visually detect early
  • Shear stress can alter morphology and yield
  • Foaming and oxygen transfer challenges
  • Requires additional equipment and sterility discipline
