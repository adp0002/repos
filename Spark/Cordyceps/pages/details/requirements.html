3. Equipment ‚Äî Purpose, Hazards & Controls

Preparation & Sterilization

  • Analytical balances: accurate massing ‚Äî calibrate; avoid vibration and drafts.
  • Mixers & blenders: homogenize substrate ‚Äî contain dust; use explosion/dust controls for fine powders.
  • Autoclave / steam sterilizer: sterilize media, glassware ‚Äî pressure/steam hazard; train operators.
  • Drying ovens / dehydrators: dry harvested product ‚Äî fire/heat risk.

Aseptic handling

  • Class II Biosafety Cabinet: sterile manipulations ‚Äî user training required to maintain airflow.
  • Laminar flow benches: for general clean work; not a substitute for BSC for biohazardous work.

Growth & Incubation

  • Incubators & environmental chambers: temperature and humidity control ‚Äî electrical and thermal safety.
  • Orbital shakers / flasks: for liquid culture expansion ‚Äî secure attachments to avoid spills.
  • Stirred tank & airlift bioreactors: controlled pH/DO ‚Äî complex maintenance; gas/pressure hazards.
  • Humidified fruiting chambers: maintain RH while filtering intake air ‚Äî microbial control essential.

Monitoring & instrumentation

  • pH meters, DO probes, temp loggers: calibration and maintenance required.
  • CO2 and hygrometers: personnel safety and culture monitoring.

Downstream & Analysis

  • Centrifuges: separate biomass ‚Äî rotor safety and balancing critical.
  • Filtration units & presses: broth clarification ‚Äî solvent compatibility if solvents are used later.
  • Lyophilizers / freeze dryers: preserve bioactives ‚Äî vacuum and cold hazard.
  • HPLC / LC‚ÄëMS: quantitative analytics ‚Äî solvent handling hazards.

PPE & safety infrastructure

  • PPE: lab coats, nitrile gloves, goggles, respirators for dust/spores.
  • Safety: eyewash, showers, spill kits, chemical storage cabinets.
  • Waste: autoclave facilities and hazardous waste segregation.

4. Human Health Risks & Controls

Biological & chemical exposures during cultivation can cause respiratory sensitization, allergic reactions, and chemical toxicity. Controls below are essential.

Biological risks

  • Spore inhalation: may trigger allergic responses or occupational asthma. Use local exhaust and respirators where dust/spores present.
  • Allergenic proteins: substrate powders (soy, insect powder) can sensitize workers; administrative controls and PPE recommended.
  • Immunocompromised staff: should avoid direct handling of cultures.

Chemical risks

  • Toxic salts: sodium selenite, concentrated copper salts ‚Äî handle in fume hood; assign restricted access.
  • Organic solvents: HPLC solvents (acetonitrile, methanol) are flammable/toxic ‚Äî store in flammable cabinet and use fume hood.
  • Disinfectants: bleach vapors and mixtures with acids release hazardous gases ‚Äî train staff and prevent mixing.

Engineering & administrative controls

  • LEVs and HEPA filtration for growth chambers and weighing stations.
  • Standardized PPE matrix and training; fit testing for respirators.
  • Medical surveillance and incident reporting for staff with respiratory symptoms.

5. Procurement, QA & Documentation

  • Material Master Sheet: CAS#, supplier, CoA, storage conditions, lot numbers, expiries.
  • Supplier qualification for biological materials (insect powders) including microbial testing and heavy metal screening.
  • Batch records: ingredient lots, preparation notes, deviations.
  • SDS library and accessible emergency contact list.

Suggested artifacts to produce

  • Risk assessment matrices for each task.
  • Waste handling and segregation SOPs (biological vs chemical vs solvent).
  • Calibration logs for balances, pH meters, probes, and analytical instruments.
